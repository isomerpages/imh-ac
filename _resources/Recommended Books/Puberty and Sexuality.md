<p>Here are some recommended books regarding puberty and sexuality among
persons with autism!</p>
<p><sub>Note: Books may be found at National Library (NLB) Singapore, selected local bookstores and online bookstores.</sub>
</p>

<div class="isomer-card-body">
<div class="isomer-card-title">Attwood, S. (2008). Making sense of sex. A Forthright Guide to Puberty,
Sex and Relationships for People with Asperger’s Syndrome.</div>
<div class="isomer-card-description">This book is a clear and accessible guide that addresses the challenges
of puberty, sex, and relationships for people with Asperger’s syndrome.
It covers important topics like hygiene, understanding relationships, consent,
and managing social situations.</div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="isomer-card-image">

</div>
<div class="isomer-card-body">
<div class="isomer-card-title">Hartman, D. (2014). Sexuality and Relationship Education for Children
and Adolescents with Autism Spectrum Disorders. Jessica Kingsley Publishers
(London).</div>
<div class="isomer-card-description">A comprehensive guide designed for educators, parents, and professionals,
offering practical lessons on sexuality and relationship education tailored
specifically for children and adolescents with autism.</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="isomer-card">
<div class="isomer-card-image">

</div>
</div>
<div class="isomer-card-body">
<div class="isomer-card-title">Nichols, S., Moravcik, G. M., &amp; Tetenbaum, S. P. (2009). Girls Growing
Up on the Autism Spectrum: What Parents and Professionals Should Know about
the Pre-teen and Teenage Years. London: Jessica Kingsley Publishers.</div>
<div class="isomer-card-description">A valuable guide focusing on the unique challenges faced by girls with
ASD during puberty, addressing issues like body changes, social dynamics,
and self-advocacy.</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="isomer-card">
<div class="isomer-card-image">

</div>
<div class="isomer-card-body">
<div class="isomer-card-title">Dundon, R. (2018). Talking with your child about their Autism Diagnosis.
A Guide for Parents. London: Jessica Kingsley Publisher.</div>
<div class="isomer-card-description">Although broader in scope, this book contains key sections on discussing
puberty, relationships, and sexuality with children on the spectrum in
a sensitive and age-appropriate manner.</div>
</div>

<div class="isomer-card-body">
<div class="isomer-card-title">Hénault, I. (2005). Asperger’s Syndrome and Sexuality: From Adolescence
through Adulthood. London: Jessica Kingsley Publishers.</div>
<div class="isomer-card-description">Focuses on navigating sexual identity, relationships, and personal boundaries
for individuals with Asperger’s syndrome, providing practical tools for
parents and professionals.</div>
</div>

<div class="isomer-card-title">Gray, C. (2015). The New Social Story Book (15th Anniversary Edition).
Future Horizons.</div>
<div class="isomer-card-description">This collection includes social stories on various topics, including some
about puberty and growing up.</div>

<div class="isomer-card-title">Wrobel M. (2005). Taking Care of Myself: A Hygiene, Puberty, and Personal
Curriculum for Young People with Autism. Future Horizons.</div>
<div class="isomer-card-description">This book uses social stories to teach children and young people with
ASD about personal care, puberty, and appropriate sexual behaviour.</div>

autism understand and manage menstruation.</div>
